Characteristics:

Large, Clear Lettering:

The emphasis is on larger and more open letters that are easier for beginners to follow. Children need to develop hand control and precision, so focusing on bold, simple strokes helps with muscle memory.

Basic Tools:

Learners at this stage are often encouraged to start with simple tools like felt-tip pens or beginner-friendly calligraphy markers rather than more advanced tools like dip pens or brushes,

Basic Strokes:

Instead of diving into full letters right away, beginners start with practicing the basic strokes that form the foundation of many calligraphy letters. These strokes include straight lines, curves, and loops.

Sans Serif Lettering:

Since serif letters often contain small decorative strokes, beginners and children may start with sans serif forms, where letters are free from additional complexities
