About Calligraphy

Calligraphy is an ancient art form that has evolved over centuries. Here, we explore its origins and different styles:

Western Calligraphy

Western calligraphy evolved from Roman scripts, with styles such as Gothic and Italic. This form of writing emphasizes elegance and precision.

Arabic Calligraphy

acha

Arabic calligraphy is rich in spiritual meaning, using styles like Diwani and Naskh. It's widely admired for its intricate and flowing design.
Oriental Calligraphy Oriental calligraphy, practiced in China and Japan, uses brushwork and ink to create flowing, artistic characters with deep cultural significance.
